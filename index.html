Layer 1 — Thesis

Why Copper Miners Should Trend Higher

The structural supply-demand case for copper is the strongest in a generation. Miners provide 2–3× leveraged exposure with equity upside.

Supply Deficit — Structural

10Mt gap by 2040

S&P Global (Jan 2026): production peaks at 33Mt in 2030, demand reaches 42Mt by 2040. Deficit = 25% of demand. New mines take 17–29 years. ICSG projects 150K+ tonne deficit in 2026.

Supply cannot respond fast enough

Demand — Multi-Vector

+50% by 2040

Four simultaneous drivers: EVs (3–4× copper vs ICE), grid/renewables, AI data centres (550 GW by 2040), and defence ($6T by 2040). Plus humanoid robots as fifth vector (~1.6Mt/yr by 2040).

All vectors scaling simultaneously

Near-Term Supply Shocks

Grasberg shutdown through Q2 2026. Kamoa-Kakula seismic disruption. Chile/Peru constrained by water, declining ore grades. Mine supply only +1.4% in 2026. Chinese smelters cutting 10%.

Active disruptions tightening now

Price Consensus

JP Morgan: $12,500/t Q2 2026 (330K t deficit). UBS: $11,000/t. Citi: $11–12K/t. Copper hit $13,240/t record Jan 2026. Structural case supports $15,000/t long-term.

Consensus $11K–$12.5K/t for 2026
Why miners over commodity

COPX delivers 2–3× leveraged beta to copper (ρ ≈ 0.7 to spot) with equity upside — operating leverage amplifies as prices rise above marginal cost. Historically, miners outperform spot by 1.5–2.5× in sustained bull markets.

Evidence Base

Why This Framework, On This Asset

Concise empirical backing for the key claims: trend-following works on commodities, entry timing matters, and COPX has the right characteristics.
ClaimEvidenceSource
Trend-following generates positive returns on commodities Time-series momentum (buying assets with positive trailing returns, shorting negative) produces significant positive returns across 58 liquid instruments over 1985–2012. Commodity futures are among the strongest performers. The premium persists because most participants cannot tolerate the behavioural cost of trend drawdowns. Moskowitz, Ooi & Pedersen (2012)
Deploying after drawdowns outperforms deploying after strong runs Strategies deployed after periods of poor performance (trailing Sharpe < 0.5) outperform those deployed after strong runs (Sharpe > 1.5) by 3–4% annualised over the next 12 months. This is strategy-level mean-reversion — returns cluster, and crowding after strong periods compresses forward returns. Clare et al. (2016), Huang et al. (2020)
Miners amplify commodity trends with operating leverage Copper miners exhibit 2–3× beta to spot copper during sustained trends due to operating leverage (fixed costs mean marginal revenue flows to equity). COPX historically delivers 1.5–2.5× the return of copper spot in trending markets, with corresponding amplified drawdowns. S&P Global (2026), Historical COPX/HG
Multi-timeframe confirmation improves trade quality Requiring alignment across multiple timeframes filters approximately 40–60% of false signals compared to single-timeframe systems. The trade-off is fewer entries but higher win rate and better risk-adjusted returns per trade. Dominic (practitioner), Faber (2007)
Position sizing is the primary risk management tool ATR-based sizing normalises risk across assets with different volatility profiles. Capping at 2% account risk per trade ensures survivability through the expected 40–60% losing trade rate inherent in trend-following. Kelly criterion analysis confirms fractional sizing maximises geometric growth. Van Tharp, Kelly (1956)

Layer 4 — Entry, Sizing & Execution (When Triggered)

Parameters for Deployment

Entry

Size
Max 2% account risk. Shares = (2% × Account) ÷ (Entry − Stop). COPX daily ATR ≈ $2.50. Scale: 50% at trigger → 25% on daily confirmation → 25% on weekly follow-through.
Stop
Initial: Below 4H Supertrend at entry, or entry − 3× daily ATR (~$7.50 below entry). Hard floor: Below Weekly Supertrend ($92.85 currently, will adjust).
Timing
US regular hours, NYSE Arca. Tightest spreads 10:00–15:30 ET. Avg daily volume ~$25M — no liquidity issue. Avoid first/last 15 min.
Layer 5 — Trade Management

Right-Tail Capture Rules

Trail
Trail with Daily Supertrend — never manually tighten. Adapts to volatility via ATR. This captures the right-tail if copper enters a sustained structural bull.
Partial
Take 30% off at 2× initial risk. Trail remaining 70% with Daily Supertrend. Consider another 20% at $100, $110 round levels.
Full exit
Daily Supertrend flips Sell while underwater. Weekly Supertrend flips — invalidates structural trend. Copper spot breaks 200d MA.
Add
Only if in profit, weekly trend strengthening, and total position within risk limits. Add on next 4H pullback within trend. Never add to a losing position.
The structural case for patience

If the S&P Global deficit thesis plays out, this is a multi-year trade. A move from $12K/t to $15K/t copper (25%) could mean 50–75% miner upside. There will be pullbacks along the way — each one is a deployment opportunity, not a reason to chase now. The thesis doesn't expire next week. Your edge is being positioned at the right entry, not being positioned first.
